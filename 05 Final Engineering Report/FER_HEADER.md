# Final Engineering Report
**Revision 1.0**

---

# Purpose

The Final Engineering Report documents the completed FCX-01 project.

Its purpose is to communicate the final vehicle, the engineering process used to develop it, and the lessons learned throughout the project.

Unlike the Engineering Workbook, which evolves continuously during development, the Final Engineering Report is a snapshot of the completed project.

---

# Objectives

The Final Engineering Report exists to:

- Summarize the completed vehicle
- Present the project's engineering process
- Document key engineering decisions
- Summarize validation and testing
- Capture lessons learned
- Document the AI-assisted engineering case study
- Serve as a professional portfolio piece

---

# Guiding Principles

The report should:

- Be technically accurate
- Focus on engineering reasoning
- Present evidence supporting major conclusions
- Communicate clearly to engineers and technically minded readers
- Emphasize vehicle-level engineering over individual components

The report should explain both **what** was built and **why** it was built that way.

---

# Expected Content

The final structure will be determined as the project matures.

Likely topics include:

- Project overview
- Requirements
- Vehicle architecture
- Major engineering decisions
- Subsystem summaries
- Manufacturing
- Validation and testing
- Performance evaluation
- Lessons learned
- AI-assisted engineering collaboration

# Revision Policy

The Final Engineering Report should be developed near the completion of the project.

It is expected to draw extensively from the Engineering Workbook, Architecture Decision Records, Engineering Logbook, and Technical Data Library.

The report is intended to communicate the completed engineering effort rather than serve as the project's primary engineering documentation.
