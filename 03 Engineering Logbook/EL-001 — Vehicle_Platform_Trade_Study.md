# Objective

Continue development of the FCX-01 donor vehicle selection trade study through literature review, engineering discussion, and expansion of the candidate solution space.

---

# Work Completed

## Literature Review

Completed a review of published information regarding the Mazda RX-7 FC and FD chassis.

Topics investigated included:

- Chassis architecture
- Suspension design
- Structural stiffness
- Weight optimization
- Power Plant Frame (PPF)
- Factory engineering philosophy

Findings were incorporated into EW-001.

---

## Broader Platform Evaluation

Expanded the trade study beyond the RX-7 platform to evaluate whether other vehicles better satisfy the project requirements.

Platforms considered included:

- Porsche 944
- Nissan S13/S14
- BMW E36
- Nissan Z32
- Toyota A70 Supra
- Toyota MR2 SW20

Evaluation focused on:

- Chassis capability
- Handling potential
- Powertrain packaging
- Serviceability
- Project suitability
- Alignment with the Project Charter

Conclusion:

No alternative platform demonstrated a clearly superior overall fit for FCX-01 compared to the RX-7 platform.

---

## Platform Capability vs. Project Execution

A distinction was introduced between two different categories of engineering evaluation.

### Platform Capability

Intrinsic characteristics that cannot be economically modified.

Examples:

- Chassis stiffness
- Suspension geometry
- Structural architecture
- Mass centralization

### Project Execution

Characteristics affecting implementation effort.

Examples:

- Donor cost
- Parts availability
- Swap maturity
- Aftermarket support
- Documentation

This distinction was incorporated into EW-001 to improve clarity and reduce bias during evaluation.

---

## Cost vs. Performance

Discussion shifted from identifying the "best chassis" toward determining the engineering value of the FD's architectural improvements.

Current understanding:

- The FD offers measurable architectural advantages.
- The FC offers lower acquisition cost and lower execution risk.
- The remaining engineering question concerns whether the FD's advantages justify its market premium.

Future work should emphasize cost-to-requirement satisfaction rather than purchase price alone.

---

## Engine Swap Research

Completed a preliminary survey of documented engine swaps.

Common swap ecosystems identified:

FC:

- LS
- K-series
- J-series
- LFX
- 1UZ
- Rotary variants

FD:

- LS
- K-series
- JZ
- LFX
- Rotary variants

Current evidence suggests the FC possesses a more mature and diverse swap ecosystem, although both platforms are well supported.

---

# Decisions

No platform selection has been made.

Current engineering position:

- The FD remains the leading platform based on intrinsic chassis capability.
- The FC remains a highly competitive alternative due to significantly lower acquisition cost and reduced execution complexity.
- Market economics are expected to determine the final platform recommendation.

# AI Collaboration Reflection

## Contributions

AI assistance was most valuable in:

- Expanding the candidate solution space beyond the RX-7.
- Organizing literature findings into a structured trade study.
- Distinguishing platform capability from project execution.
- Identifying recurring engineering themes across multiple evidence sources.
- Challenging assumptions through comparative analysis.

## Potential Limitations

As evidence increasingly favored the FD, there was a risk of prematurely converging on a preferred solution.
The model repeatedly attempted to blow up the scope of the project. User had to refocus and narrow the scope several times.
This was mitigated by deliberately evaluating strong alternative platforms and reframing the decision around engineering value rather than absolute performance.

## Lessons Learned

Separating intrinsic platform capability from implementation complexity substantially improved the clarity of the trade study.

Future engineering decisions should continue to distinguish between characteristics inherent to the platform and those that can be modified through design effort or investment.
