# EW-001 — Vehicle Platform Trade Study
## FC RX-7 vs. FD RX-7

**Revision:** Draft 0.3

---

# Objective

Determine the most appropriate donor platform for FCX-01 by evaluating the Mazda FC and FD RX-7 against the engineering requirements established in the Project Charter.

This document evaluates each chassis as an engineering platform rather than as a collector vehicle.

---

# 1. Project Requirements

The selected platform should maximize the following project objectives.

| Requirement | Priority |
|-------------|----------|
| Balanced handling | Critical |
| Reliability potential | Critical |
| Cost efficiency | Critical |
| Daily drivability | High |
| Track capability | High |
| OEM-like refinement | High |
| Engineering documentation value | Medium |
| Serviceability | Medium |
| Aftermarket support | Medium |
| Weight optimization | High |

---

# 2. Candidate Platforms

## Candidate A

**Mazda RX-7 FC (1986–1991)**

Characteristics

- Second-generation RX-7
- Front MacPherson strut suspension
- Independent rear suspension
- Lower acquisition cost
- Extensive aftermarket support

---

## Candidate B

**Mazda RX-7 FD (1993–1995 USDM)**

Characteristics

- Third-generation RX-7
- Aluminum-intensive double wishbone front suspension
- Increased chassis rigidity
- Reduced body mass
- Higher acquisition cost

---

# 3. Evaluation Methodology

Engineering conclusions shall be supported by documented evidence and assigned a confidence level reflecting the quality and completeness of the available information.

This study draws upon:

- Manufacturer engineering documentation
- Published technical specifications
- Academic and technical literature
- Community experience
- Market observations
- Engineering analysis

---

## 3.1 Evidence Hierarchy

Engineering evidence shall be weighted according to the following hierarchy.

| Level | Source | Typical Examples |
|-------|--------|------------------|
| A | Manufacturer documentation | Factory service manuals, engineering papers, SAE publications |
| B | Academic / Technical Literature | Textbooks, peer-reviewed papers, instrumented testing |
| C | Community Consensus | RX7Club, experienced owner reports, recurring documented experience |
| D | Individual Reports | Build threads, isolated forum posts |
| E | FCX-01 Project Data | Measurements, testing and validation performed during this project |

When conflicting evidence exists, higher-level sources shall generally take precedence unless contradicted by FCX-01 validation testing.

---

## 3.2 Engineering Evaluation Framework

To distinguish inherent platform quality from implementation effort, evaluation criteria are divided into two categories.

### Platform Capability

These criteria evaluate the inherent engineering characteristics of the chassis. They represent attributes that are difficult or uneconomical to modify after vehicle acquisition.

Examples include:

- Chassis architecture
- Structural stiffness
- Suspension geometry
- Weight optimization
- Mass centralization
- Powertrain packaging

### Project Execution

These criteria evaluate the effort required to transform the donor vehicle into FCX-01.

Examples include:

- Acquisition cost
- Parts availability
- Engine swap ecosystem maturity
- Manufacturing complexity
- Serviceability
- Long-term project cost

Both categories shall be evaluated independently before reaching an overall engineering recommendation.

---

## 3.3 Cost vs. Performance Philosophy

Purchase price alone is not an engineering objective.

The preferred platform is the one that achieves the project requirements with the lowest total engineering and financial cost.

Accordingly, this study evaluates both platform capability and implementation effort.

# 4. Engineering Comparison

## Part A — Platform Capability

---

## 4.1 Chassis Engineering

### Literature Review

Mazda's development program for the FD prioritized increasing structural stiffness while simultaneously reducing body weight.

Published Mazda engineering literature reports approximately:

- 20% increase in body bending stiffness
- 15% increase in torsional stiffness
- 30 kg reduction in body structure weight

Major architectural improvements include:

- Redesigned body structure
- Improved load paths
- Extensive structural optimization
- Power Plant Frame (PPF)
- Revised suspension mounting architecture

### Research Gaps

- Absolute torsional stiffness values
- Factory chassis section diagrams
- Suspension pickup point stiffness

### Engineering Discussion

Body stiffness is an architectural characteristic that cannot be economically reproduced through aftermarket modification.

### Confidence

High

### Engineering Position

FD currently favored.

---

## 4.2 Suspension Engineering

### Literature Review

**FC**

- Front MacPherson strut suspension
- Independent rear suspension
- Excellent aftermarket support

**FD**

- Aluminum-intensive double wishbone front suspension
- Improved rear suspension geometry
- Reduced unsprung mass
- Improved camber control
- Power Plant Frame improves driveline rigidity

### Research Gaps

- Roll center comparison
- Camber gain comparison
- Anti-dive geometry
- Anti-squat geometry
- Bump steer comparison

### Engineering Discussion

The FD's advantages are primarily geometric rather than component-based. Modern dampers and bushings substantially improve the FC but cannot fully reproduce the FD's suspension architecture.

### Confidence

High

### Engineering Position

FD currently favored.

---

## 4.3 Weight & Mass Centralization

### Literature Review

Mazda's development objectives emphasized reducing both vehicle weight and polar moment of inertia.

Published engineering literature indicates:

- Approximately 30 kg reduction in body structure weight
- Near 50:50 weight distribution
- Improved mass centralization
- Lower polar moment through packaging optimization

### Research Gaps

- Unsprung mass comparison
- Center of gravity height
- Measured weight distribution by trim level

### Engineering Discussion

Vehicle dynamics are expected to be influenced more by mass distribution and rotational inertia than curb weight alone.

### Confidence

Medium

### Engineering Position

FD currently favored.

---

## 4.4 Powertrain Packaging

### Literature Review

Current literature is insufficient.

Existing documentation primarily consists of completed engine swap projects and community build threads.

Candidate powertrains include:

### Research Gaps

- Steering rack clearance
- Firewall clearance
- Transmission tunnel dimensions

- Packaging comparison between FC and FD

### Engineering Discussion

Packaging complexity directly affects manufacturability, serviceability and project cost.

### Confidence

Low

### Engineering Position

## 4.5 Corrosion & Structural Integrity

### Literature Review

Both platforms exhibit well-documented corrosion in structural areas.

**FC**

- Front frame rails
- Rockers
- Rear wheel arches
- Spare tire well

**FD**

- Frame rails
- Rockers
- Rear wheel arches
- Suspension pickup points

### Research Gaps

- Relative corrosion severity
- Geographic trends
- Restoration cost comparison

### Engineering Discussion

Structural corrosion is expected to influence donor suitability more than cosmetic condition.

### Confidence

Low

### Engineering Position

Insufficient evidence.

---

## 4.6 Architectural Summary

Certain engineering characteristics are fundamental to the chassis and cannot be economically replicated through aftermarket modification.

| Characteristic | FC | FD | Retrofit Feasible |
|----------------|----|----|-------------------|
| Body stiffness | Lower | Higher | No |
| Suspension geometry | Good | Superior | Partial |
| Power Plant Frame | No | Yes | No |
| Mass centralization | Good | Excellent | No |
| Body structural optimization | Earlier generation | New design | No |

These characteristics should receive greater weighting than differences that can reasonably be addressed through aftermarket upgrades.

## 4.7 Acquisition Cost

### Literature Review

**FC**

- Lower collector value
- Larger supply of project vehicles
- Lower average asking prices

**FD**

- Higher collector value
- Significantly higher average market prices
- Salvage and auction markets occasionally narrow the acquisition cost gap

### Research Gaps

- Marketplace survey
- Bring a Trailer sales analysis
- Cars & Bids sales analysis
- Copart observations
- Effective donor cost after part-out

### Engineering Discussion

Acquisition cost is expected to be the largest economic driver of platform selection.

### Confidence

Medium

### Engineering Position

FC currently favored.

---

## 4.8 Parts Availability

### Literature Review

**FC**

- Extensive aftermarket support
- Large used parts supply
- Lower replacement component costs

**FD**

- Strong enthusiast support
- Increasing collector demand
- Certain OEM components becoming scarce

### Research Gaps

- Current aftermarket pricing
- OEM availability survey
- Long-term parts outlook

### Engineering Discussion

Long-term parts availability directly affects maintenance cost and project sustainability.

### Confidence

Medium

### Engineering Position

FC currently favored.

---

## 4.9 Engine Swap Ecosystem

### Literature Review

Both platforms support numerous documented engine conversions.

**FC**

- Extensive documentation for LS, JZ and 1UZ swaps
- Large body of community knowledge
- Numerous custom fabrication examples

**FD**

- Extensive documentation for LS and JZ swaps
- Strong commercial support for LS conversions
- Fewer documented non-traditional swaps

### Research Gaps

- Modern automatic transmission integration
- Drive-by-wire implementation
- CAN bus integration
- LFX ecosystem maturity
- K-series ecosystem maturity

### Engineering Discussion

A mature swap ecosystem reduces engineering risk by decreasing fabrication effort, troubleshooting time and integration uncertainty.

This criterion evaluates documentation maturity rather than engine suitability.

### Confidence

Medium

### Engineering Position

Slight advantage: FC.

---

### Confidence

### Engineering Position

Insufficient evidence.

### Current Engineering Position

The FD currently demonstrates a measurable engineering advantage as a vehicle platform.

However, insufficient evidence exists to determine whether those advantages justify the additional implementation cost.

Further market research, packaging analysis and project cost modeling are required before a platform recommendation can be made.
