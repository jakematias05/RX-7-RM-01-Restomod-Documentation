# EW-001 — Vehicle Platform Trade Study
## FC RX-7 vs. FD RX-7
Revision: Draft 0.1

---

# Objective

Determine the most appropriate donor platform for FCX-01 by evaluating the Mazda FC and FD RX-7 against the engineering requirements established in the Project Charter.

This document evaluates the chassis as engineering platforms rather than as collector vehicles.

---

# 1. Project Requirements

The selected platform should maximize the following project objectives.

| Requirement | Priority |
|-------------|----------|
| Balanced handling | Critical |
| Reliability potential | Critical |
| Cost efficiency | Critical |
| Daily drivability | High |
| Track capability | High |
| OEM-like refinement | High |
| Engineering documentation value | Medium |
| Serviceability | Medium |
| Aftermarket support | Medium |
| Weight optimization | High |

---

# 2. Candidate Platforms

## Candidate A

Mazda RX-7 FC (1986–1991)

Characteristics

- Second-generation RX-7
- Front MacPherson strut suspension
- Independent rear suspension
- Lower market value
- Extensive aftermarket support

---

## Candidate B

Mazda RX-7 FD (1993–1995 USDM)

Characteristics

- Third-generation RX-7
- Aluminum-intensive double wishbone front suspension
- Improved chassis rigidity
- Lower vehicle mass
- Higher acquisition cost

---

# 3. Evaluation Methodology

Each engineering requirement will be evaluated using:

- Published technical specifications

# 4. Engineering Comparison

---

## 4.1 Acquisition Cost

### Evidence

**FC**

- Typical project cars appear significantly less expensive.
- Larger supply on local marketplaces.
- Lower collector value.

**FD**

- Collector values are substantially higher.
- High-mileage automatic examples occasionally sell at considerably lower prices.
- Salvage and auction markets may narrow the price gap.

### Data Required

- Marketplace survey
- Bring a Trailer sales
- Cars & Bids sales
- Copart observations

### Discussion

The cost difference is likely the largest factor affecting platform selection.

### Confidence

Medium

### Preliminary Assessment

Advantage: FC

---

## 4.2 Chassis Engineering

### Evidence

FC

- Proven chassis.
- Good weight distribution.
- MacPherson strut front suspension.

FD

- Significantly newer platform.
- Improved torsional rigidity.
- Double wishbone front suspension.
- Extensive aluminum suspension components.
- Power Plant Frame.

### Data Required

- Published torsional rigidity values
- Suspension geometry comparison
- Factory engineering documentation

### Discussion

The FD possesses architectural improvements that cannot easily be replicated through aftermarket modification.

### Confidence

High

### Preliminary Assessment

Advantage: FD

---

## 4.3 Suspension Potential

### Evidence

FC

- Excellent aftermarket support.
- Modern dampers and bushings greatly improve performance.

FD

- Superior factory geometry.
- Lower unsprung mass.
- Better camber control.

### Data Required

- Suspension kinematics
- Roll center comparison
- Alignment specifications

### Discussion

Determine whether modern aftermarket components sufficiently close the performance gap.

### Confidence

Medium

### Preliminary Assessment

Leaning FD

# 7. Preliminary Conclusions

No recommendation has been made.

Current evidence suggests:

Advantages favoring the FC

- Lower acquisition cost
- Better aftermarket support
- Greater modification freedom
- Lower replacement component cost

Advantages favoring the FD

- Superior chassis architecture
- Better suspension geometry
- Lower weight
- Greater OEM refinement

Additional evidence is required before a platform recommendation is made.
