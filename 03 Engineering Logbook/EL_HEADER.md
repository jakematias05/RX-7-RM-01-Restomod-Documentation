# Engineering Logbook
**Revision 1.0**

---

# Purpose

The Engineering Logbook is the chronological record of FCX-01 development.

Its purpose is to document engineering work as it occurs, providing a history of the project's progress, discoveries, challenges, and next steps.

Unlike the Engineering Workbook, which defines the current engineering baseline, the Logbook records the process that produced it.

---

# Objectives

The Engineering Logbook exists to:

- Record engineering work completed
- Document discoveries and observations
- Capture unresolved questions
- Track project progress
- Provide continuity between engineering sessions
- Preserve context that may not belong elsewhere
- Continuously improve the engineering process through reflection

---

# Entry Format

Each engineering session should begin a new log entry.

## Date

Session date.

---

## Summary

Brief overview of the session.

---

## Work Performed

Major engineering tasks completed.

---

## Key Decisions

List significant decisions made.

Reference ADRs where applicable.

---

## Observations

Interesting findings, unexpected results, lessons learned, or technical concerns.

---

## Open Items

Questions, risks, or work requiring future attention.

---

## Next Session

Recommended starting point for the next engineering session.

---

## Daily Retrospective

The purpose of the retrospective is to improve future engineering work within FCX-01.

### Engineering Reflection

- What went well?
- What could have gone better?
- Were any assumptions challenged?
- What information was missing?
- How can the next engineering session be improved?

### AI Self-Assessment

Reflect on AI's contribution to the session.

Consider:

- Where AI provided meaningful engineering value
- Where AI introduced unnecessary complexity
- Where AI should have requested additional information
- How AI can better support future engineering sessions

### Human Self-Assessment

Reflect on the engineering process from the human perspective.

Consider:

- Decisions that deserve additional review
- Opportunities to improve documentation
- Better ways to communicate requirements
- Process improvements for future sessions

### Collaboration Notes

Record observations regarding the interaction between human engineers and AI.

These notes contribute to the project's case study on AI-assisted engineering but should remain focused on improving future engineering work.

---

# Writing Guidelines

Logbook entries should:

- Be concise.
- Be chronological.
- Record events rather than conclusions.
- Reference other project documents instead of duplicating them.
- Prioritize capturing information while it is still fresh.

The Logbook is intended to reduce reliance on memory between engineering sessions.

---

# Relationship to Other Documents

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Engineering Workbook | Defines the current engineering baseline. |
| Architecture Decision Records | Explain why significant decisions were made. |
| Engineering Logbook | Records the project's engineering history. |
| Technical Data Library | Stores engineering artifacts and supporting files. |

---

# Revision Policy

Logbook entries should not be substantially rewritten after they are recorded.

Corrections or follow-up notes may be appended when necessary, but the historical record should remain intact.

The Engineering Logbook is intended to accurately reflect the project's development as it occurred.
