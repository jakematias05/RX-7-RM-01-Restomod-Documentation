## Overview

**FCX-01** is an engineering-driven restomod development project based on the Mazda FC RX-7 chassis.

The objective is to develop a modern interpretation of the FC platform using a disciplined vehicle development process inspired by professional automotive engineering practices.

The project focuses not only on the completed vehicle, but also on documenting the engineering process used to create it: requirements definition, system architecture, trade studies, design decisions, validation methods, and lessons learned.

FCX-01 serves as both:

- A personal engineering project
- A demonstration of systems engineering methodology
- A platform for collaborative engineering development
- A case study in AI-assisted engineering workflows

---

# Project Goals

## Vehicle Development Goals

The goal of FCX-01 is to create a vehicle that represents a modern factory evolution of the FC RX-7 platform.

Key objectives include:

- Balanced and predictable handling
- Daily-driver reliability
- OEM-level refinement
- Track-day capability
- Long-term maintainability
- Serviceable design
- Cost-conscious engineering
- Cohesive vehicle integration

Target characteristics include:

- Approximately 300–400 horsepower
- Modern automatic transmission with paddle shifting
- Improved electronic integration
- Lightweight construction
- OEM+ styling
- Improved aerodynamic efficiency

The project does not prioritize maximum horsepower or performance metrics at the expense of overall vehicle behavior.

The objective is a complete, integrated vehicle system.

---

# Systems Engineering Approach

FCX-01 applies a systems engineering methodology to vehicle development.

Engineering decisions are guided by:

- Clearly defined requirements
- Vehicle-level thinking
- Evidence-based decision making
- Documented trade studies
- Design reviews
- Validation planning
- Controlled documentation

Subsystems are not developed independently. Every decision is evaluated based on its impact on the complete vehicle system.

The project emphasizes understanding:

- Why a design decision was made
- What alternatives were considered
- What assumptions were made
- How the decision will be validated

# AI-Assisted Engineering Research

FCX-01 is also a study of how artificial intelligence can support engineering development within a small team.

AI tools are used as engineering collaborators to assist with:

- Systems engineering analysis
- Design reviews
- Brainstorming
- Trade studies
- Technical research
- Documentation
- Engineering calculations
- Knowledge organization

Human engineers remain responsible for:

- Defining requirements
- Making engineering judgments
- Approving decisions
- Performing validation
- Accepting final designs

The goal is not to replace engineering expertise, but to investigate how AI can improve engineering quality, efficiency, and collaboration.

---

# Research Objectives

Throughout the project, AI-assisted engineering interactions will be documented and evaluated.

Areas of study include:

- Where AI provides meaningful engineering value
- Where AI recommendations require additional scrutiny
- How AI can improve technical documentation
- How AI can support design reviews
- Best practices for human-AI engineering collaboration

The project aims to develop practical insight into the role of AI in complex engineering workflows.

# Development Philosophy

FCX-01 follows several core principles:

**Requirements Drive Design**  
Components and solutions are selected because they satisfy engineering requirements.

**Engineering Before Preference**  
Decisions should be supported by evidence, analysis, testing, or documented experience.

**Document the Reasoning**  
Engineering decisions should capture context, alternatives, justification, and validation plans.

**Embrace Revision**  
Changing a design based on better evidence is considered successful engineering.

# Long-Term Vision

FCX-01 aims to demonstrate that a small engineering team can execute a complex vehicle development program by combining:

- Systems engineering discipline
- Digital engineering tools
- Modern documentation practices
- AI-assisted collaboration

Success will be measured not only by the completed vehicle, but also by the quality of the engineering process used to create it.

The final outcome will be both a vehicle and a documented case study exploring the future of human-AI collaboration in engineering.
